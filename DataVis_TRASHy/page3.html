Our Design Process

This fictitious project, commissioned by Shanghai Government, aims at outlining the current situation of the waste management of the city, with a focus on its different districts, and offering a strong visual starting point for the authority’s intervention.

The iterative process of the project consists of three strongly interrelated phases: Data collection, Data analysis and Data visualisation. Data visualisation was used to analyse the data in both ways, proving assumptions through visualisation and creating new hypothesis from the visualisation.

Based on the Shanghai Government’s brief, we started to get a holistic understanding of the case and its context. Our research included articles about Shanghai’s current waste management, research papers about waste management, and phone calls with employees of the Ministry of Environment. The initial general research helped us to create a visual overview of the official waste management system of Shanghai. This was essential to have a common understanding of the subject.

In the following data collection, we looked for data of each actor and their interrelations inside the system. We searched for and gathered data on two different layers, the city and the district level. As the raw data was not accessible, we needed to clean and merge data from different sources.

The gathered data enabled the first data analysis. Both statistical analysis and data visualisation let us test the first hypothesis and generate new ones. The emerging findings led us again to new data collection and research. In this ongoing process, the data facilitated the classification of the different districts according to their waste management and their demography. Afterwards, we started to compare districts with similar demographic profiles among their waste management efficiency, in order to find gaps or improvements points.

Finally, thanks to this visual comparison, we were able to show, in every graphic comparison, the positive directions that could be taken and adopted by those districts which show a deficiency in the system. Therefore, we believe that our visual synthesis could serve as starting point for the Government intervention to improve the waste management of the entire city.
